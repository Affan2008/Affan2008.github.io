Unix

Unix is a family of multitasking, multiuser operating systems that derive from the original AT&T Unix. Development was originally started in 1969 at the Bell Labs research center and was initially intended for internal use. In late 1970s, AT&T licensed Unix for use to outside parties, leading to a variety Unix variants such as BSD, SunOS (later Solaris), HP-UX, and Xenix (source: Wikipedia).
